Privacy Policy

Last updated: 6 July 2026. Edit this date whenever you change the policy.

1. Who this policy covers

This Privacy Policy explains how Shipwright Talent ("we", "us") collects and uses personal data when you visit this website or contact us through it. We are established in the Netherlands. Once registered, our Chamber of Commerce (KVK) number will be listed here: pending registration.

2. What we collect

  • Contact form submissions: name, email address, channel or company name, and whatever you write in the message field. Submitting the form on this site currently opens your own email client with a pre-filled message. The message is sent from your email account to ours, the same as any email.
  • Direct emails: if you email us directly, we receive whatever your message contains, including your email address and anything you choose to share (for example, channel statistics, audience data, or contract terms).
  • Cookie preference: a small local-storage entry on your device recording whether you accepted or declined the cookie notice. This does not identify you personally and is not shared with anyone.
  • We do not currently use analytics, advertising, or tracking cookies. If that changes, this section will be updated to name the tool, what it collects, and how to opt out, before it goes live.

3. Why we process it

  • To respond to inquiries from creators and brands (legal basis: taking steps prior to a contract, or our legitimate interest in running the business).
  • To manage a representation agreement or brand contract once one exists (legal basis: performance of a contract).
  • To meet legal or tax obligations once we are formally registered (legal basis: legal obligation).

4. Who we share it with

We do not sell personal data. We may share it with service providers strictly to run the business, for example an email provider, e-signature tool (such as DocuSign or HelloSign), or accounting software, once those are in place. Each provider only receives what it needs to perform its function.

5. How long we keep it

Inquiries that don't lead to a working relationship are kept only as long as needed to respond and follow up, and are deleted after a reasonable period if nothing progresses (target: 12 months). Data tied to an active or past representation agreement or brand contract is kept for as long as Dutch tax and civil record-keeping obligations require, typically up to 7 years for financial records once we are registered.

6. Your rights

Under the GDPR, you can ask us to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Correct inaccurate data
  • Delete your data, where we're not required to keep it for legal reasons
  • Restrict or object to certain processing
  • Receive a copy of your data in a portable format

To exercise any of these, email hello@example.com. You can also lodge a complaint with the Dutch Data Protection Authority (Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens) at autoriteitpersoonsgegevens.nl.

7. Cookies

This site currently sets one local-storage entry to remember your cookie banner choice, and nothing else. It does not use tracking, analytics, or advertising cookies. If you'd rather not have even that, you can decline the banner or clear your browser's local storage for this site at any time.

8. Where data is hosted

This site is hosted on GitHub Pages. Contact form emails are sent via your own email provider once you connect a real send address.

9. Contact

Questions about this policy: hello@example.com.

This policy is a template for launch and is not a substitute for legal advice. Review it, and update the bracketed sections, before relying on it, and again once you register with the KVK or start collecting more than basic contact-form data.
