Why overlanding, why now

Creator content already decides how people shop for vehicles and gear.

Automotive marketing is shifting toward the creators buyers already trust — overlanding sits at the center of that shift, with almost no one representing the creators themselves.

Category

A proven model, an open niche

Creator-side talent representation is established in beauty, gaming and family content, typically at 10–30% commission. Automotive has largely been served only by agencies working for brands, not creators.

Audience

High-intent viewers

Overlanding audiences watch long-form build and travel content and buy the gear featured in it — a rare combination of engagement and purchase intent for a YouTube niche.

Fit

Gear brands are already spending

Recovery equipment, roof-top tents, suspension and navigation electronics are categories that already run active creator marketing budgets — they're simply hard to reach without a specialist in between.

How a deal actually moves

From first call to both of you getting paid.

The same route every time — no surprises in the middle of a deal.

Discovery call

We talk about your channel, audience and the kind of sponsorships that actually fit — no commitment either way.

Representation agreement & media kit

A short, plain-language agreement, then we build a media kit from your own channel data — stats, audience, past work.

Brand introductions

We approach relevant brands directly, negotiate terms and rates on your behalf, and bring you only the deals worth your time.

Contract & delivery

Deliverables, approval windows and payment terms are set in writing before filming starts. You make the content; we manage the logistics.

Paid together

You're paid your agreed rate once the brand's funds clear. Our commission only ever comes out of a deal we closed.
